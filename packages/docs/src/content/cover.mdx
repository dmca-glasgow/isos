Title, author, ORCID link, date and abstract can optionally be displayed at the top of a document:

<CodeSnippet
  latex={String.raw`
    \documentclass{article}
    \usepackage{orcidlink}
    \begin{document}
    \title{My LaTeX Document}
    \date{June 2025}
    \author{Alison Carefully \orcidlink{0000-0002-1825-0097}}
    \maketitle

    \begin{abstract}
    My abstract
    \end{abstract}

    \section{Introduction}

    Paragraph

    \end{document}

`}
/>

For multiple authors, the `authblk` package can be used like so:

<CodeSnippet
  latex={String.raw`
    \documentclass{article}
    \usepackage{authblk}
    \usepackage{orcidlink}
    \begin{document}
    \title{My LaTeX Document}

    \author[1]{Alison Carefully \orcidlink{0000-0002-1825-0097}}
    \author[2]{Ivor Question \orcidlink{0000-0002-1825-0097}}
    \affil[1]{University of X}
    \affil[2]{University of Y}

    \begin{abstract}
    My abstract
    \end{abstract}

    \section{Introduction}

    Paragraph

    \maketitle

    \end{document}

`}
/>

In the example above, you may notice `\maketitle` has not inserted the title below the section. In an effort for consistent structure to the document, the title, author and abstract are always placed at the top and the `\maketitle` macro is ignored.
